    GOAL SETTING

    Goal setting involves the development of an action plan designed to motive and guide a person or group of people towards a goal. Goal setting can be guided by goal-setting criteria (or rules) like the "SMART" criteria. Goal setting is a major component of personal-development and management.

    PURPOSE OF GOAL SETTING

    Goal setting gives you a long-term vision and short-term motivation. It focuses on your acquisition of knowledge and helps you to organize your time and your resources so that you can make the most of your life. Goal setting helps trigger new behaviors, guides your focus, and helps you sustain that momentum in life. It also helps align your focus and promote a sense of self-mastery and regulation.

    goal

    A goal is an idea of the future desired result that a person or group of people envision, plan, and commit to achieving. People endeavor to reach goals within a certain period of time by setting deadlines but sometimes it depends on the goal that you have in mind. For example; I want to become a teacher someday, of course, it wouldn't be easy to achieve because it takes a lot of time to become a teacher, however by doing small things like studying hard you can achieve it step by step.

    SMART CRITERIA

    SMART (criteria) is an acronym that stands for specific, measurable, attainable or achievable, relevant or realistic, and time-bound or timely. Therefore, if you want to know if a goal is smart then you have to identify whether these characteristics are present to that specific goal. A smart goal helps you to maintain your focus and give your efforts to the fullest.
